Our History

Bithash was founded in 2021 during a pivotal moment for cryptocurrency. Bitcoin reached an all-time high of $69,000, China banned mining operations, and institutional adoption began accelerating. The founders, a group of hardware engineers and finance professionals, identified a clear gap in the market: retail investors had no reliable way to access Bitcoin mining or crypto lending.

We built Bithash to solve this problem. Today, we operate mining farms across North America, South America, and Northern Europe with over 12,000 ASIC miners. Our total hashrate exceeds 450 petahashes per second. We serve over 3 million users in more than 120 countries.

Mining Infrastructure

Bithash owns and operates all of its mining hardware. Our fleet includes Bitmain Antminer S21 Pro, MicroBT Whatsminer M66S, and Canaan Avalon A1566 units. We secure long-term power purchase agreements at fixed rates: $0.035/kWh in Paraguay (hydroelectric), $0.042/kWh in Texas (flare gas), and $0.038/kWh in Norway (hydro). These contracts are fixed for five years, insulating our users from energy price volatility.

When you purchase a cloud mining contract on Bithash, you receive daily payouts based on actual block rewards minus disclosed operational costs. Every payout is verifiable through our block explorer integration.

Bitcoin as Digital Gold

Bitcoin has a fixed supply of 21 million coins, making it the hardest asset ever created. Mining Bitcoin is analogous to mining physical gold, but with verifiable, programmable, and portable properties. Bithash allows users to acquire Bitcoin at production cost without purchasing hardware or managing energy contracts.

Since launch, Bithash users have accumulated over 2,800 Bitcoin through our cloud mining contracts. Each Bitcoin halving reduces the flow of new supply, making existing holdings more valuable over time.

Bitcoin-Backed Loans (Up to 3x Holdings)

Bithash offers Bitcoin-backed loans up to three times your total holdings. Users deposit Bitcoin as collateral and receive stablecoin loans (USDT, USDC, DAI) at up to 300% loan-to-value. Interest rates start at 6% APR. Mining rewards continue accumulating daily and can be used to pay down interest automatically.

Unlike DeFi protocols that limit LTV to 50-60%, Bithash offers a hybrid model with manual collateral monitoring and 48-hour margin call warnings. We have processed over 1,200 loans since 2022 with zero involuntary liquidations. The 3x loan product launched in early 2026 and has funded over $15 million in stablecoin loans.

Trading Platform

Bithash includes an embedded trading engine supporting spot trading for over 50 cryptocurrencies including Bitcoin, Ethereum, Solana, and major stablecoins. The platform aggregates liquidity from multiple tier-1 exchanges and includes DEX connectivity to Uniswap, PancakeSwap, and Curve. Cross-chain bridges support Arbitrum, Optimism, Polygon, and BNB Chain.

The trading engine executes orders in under 500 milliseconds and has maintained zero downtime since mainnet launch in 2022. Users can move instantly between mining wallets, loan collateral wallets, and trading wallets without waiting for blockchain confirmations.

Halving Cycle Strategy

Bitcoin's next halving is projected for April 2028, reducing block rewards from 3.125 BTC to 1.5625 BTC. Bithash allows users to lock hashpower contracts for 12, 24, or 36 months, hedging against rising difficulty. Our smart contract architecture automatically recalculates daily payouts based on real-time network hashrate and block reward.

Over 500,000 of our 3 million users run auto-compounding strategies, reinvesting mining rewards into additional hashpower. Average annualized yields range from 18% to 35% depending on Bitcoin price and network difficulty.

Compliance & Transparency

Bithash publishes monthly proof-of-reserves via Merkle tree audits from third-party firms. We are registered as a money services business in the United States and Canada with full KYC/AML protocols. Our governance token (BTH) allows holders to vote on maintenance fee caps, asset listings, and loan interest rates.

Bithash has survived two major market drawdowns: the 2022 FTX collapse and the 2024 post-halving shakeout. We publish monthly operations reports including energy costs, hardware failure rates, and pool performance metrics.
