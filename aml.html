Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Policy

Bithash maintains a strict zero-tolerance policy against money laundering, terrorist financing, and all forms of financial crime. This document outlines our comprehensive compliance framework in accordance with global regulatory standards.

Last Updated:

1. Introduction

Bithash operates with the highest standards of legal and regulatory compliance. We implement rigorous Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Counter-Terrorist Financing (CTF) measures to detect and prevent illicit financial activities through our platform.

Policy Scope: This policy applies to all users, employees, contractors, and third-party service providers interacting with Bithash services globally.

2. Regulatory Compliance

Bithash complies with all applicable AML/CTF laws and regulations, including but not limited to:

  • Financial Action Task Force (FATF) Recommendations
  • Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) as amended by the USA PATRIOT Act
  • EU Fourth and Fifth Anti-Money Laundering Directives (4AMLD/5AMLD)
  • Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (FinCEN) regulations
  • Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) sanctions programs
  • Local regulations in all jurisdictions where we operate

We maintain relationships with regulatory bodies and law enforcement agencies to facilitate information sharing when required by law.

3. User Identification & KYC Procedures

3.1 Mandatory Verification

All users must complete our Know Your Customer (KYC) verification process before accessing core platform services, including:

  • Deposits exceeding $1,000 USD equivalent
  • Withdrawals of any amount
  • Investment plan participation
  • Referral program withdrawals

3.2 Document Requirements

We collect and verify the following information for all users:

  • Individual Users:
    • Full legal name
    • Date of birth
    • Residential address
    • Government-issued photo ID (passport, driver's license, national ID)
    • Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement)
    • Source of funds documentation when required
  • Corporate Users:
    • Certificate of incorporation
    • Articles of Association
    • Register of beneficial owners (25%+ ownership)
    • Proof of directors' identities
    • Proof of company address

Important: Providing false identification documents or misrepresenting source of funds constitutes fraud and will result in immediate account termination and reporting to authorities.

3.3 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

Additional verification measures apply to:

  • Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
  • High-risk jurisdictions (as classified by FATF)
  • Transactions exceeding $10,000 USD equivalent
  • Unusual or suspicious activity patterns

4. Risk-Based Approach

Bithash employs a dynamic risk assessment framework that evaluates users and transactions based on multiple factors:

4.1 Risk Categories

  • Customer Risk: PEP status, jurisdiction, business nature
  • Product Risk: Anonymity features, transaction velocity
  • Geographic Risk: High-risk countries, sanctions lists
  • Channel Risk: IP anomalies, device fingerprinting

4.2 Monitoring Thresholds

Automated systems flag transactions for review when they meet any of the following criteria:

  • Single transactions exceeding $5,000 USD equivalent
  • Aggregate monthly transactions exceeding $20,000 USD equivalent
  • Rapid deposit/withdrawal patterns (churning)
  • Transactions involving mixing services or privacy coins
  • Geographic inconsistencies (e.g., IP vs. stated residency)

5. Ongoing Monitoring

Bithash maintains continuous surveillance of all account activity through:

5.1 Transaction Monitoring System

Our proprietary system analyzes transactions in real-time using:

  • Behavioral pattern recognition
  • Network analysis (common counterparties)
  • Blockchain analytics (cluster identification)
  • Sanctions/PEP screening

5.2 Suspicious Activity Indicators

Examples of activity that triggers enhanced review:

  • Structuring transactions to avoid reporting thresholds
  • Rapid movement of funds without economic purpose
  • Use of anonymous or high-risk wallet addresses
  • Inconsistent user behavior with stated profile
  • Attempts to obscure fund origins (layering)

6. Reporting Obligations

6.1 Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs)

Bithash files SARs with appropriate authorities when we detect:

  • Known or suspected violations of law
  • Transactions involving illegal proceeds
  • Attempts to evade reporting requirements
  • No apparent lawful purpose for transactions

Confidentiality: Users are not notified when SARs are filed or when accounts are restricted due to compliance concerns.

6.2 Asset Freezes

We immediately freeze accounts and assets when:

  • Directed by court order or regulatory mandate
  • Matches appear on sanctions lists
  • Credible evidence of criminal activity exists

7. Prohibited Activities

The following activities are strictly forbidden on Bithash:

  • Use of stolen funds or proceeds of crime
  • Terrorist financing or sanctions evasion
  • Operation of unlicensed money services businesses
  • Use of mixing/tumbling services
  • Transactions with darknet markets
  • Identity fraud or misrepresentation
  • Shell company activity
  • Tax evasion strategies

Consequences: Violations result in immediate account termination, forfeiture of funds, and reporting to financial intelligence units and law enforcement agencies worldwide.

8. Staff Training & System Safeguards

8.1 Employee Requirements

All Bithash personnel receive:

  • Annual AML/CTF certification training
  • Background checks prior to employment
  • Ongoing education on emerging threats

8.2 Technical Controls

Our security infrastructure includes:

  • Chainalysis Reactor for blockchain forensics
  • Jumio for identity verification
  • ComplyAdvantage for sanctions/PEP screening
  • Custom risk scoring algorithms
  • Secure document storage with 256-bit encryption

All systems integrate with our secure backend infrastructure for seamless compliance monitoring.

9. Policy Updates

Bithash reserves the right to modify this AML Policy at any time to comply with evolving regulations. Users will be notified of material changes through:

  • Email notifications to registered addresses
  • Dashboard announcements
  • Updated policy publication date

Continued use of Bithash services constitutes acceptance of the current AML Policy terms.
